Where This Model Breaks

Single-closer risk. Chris carries the entire closing capacity in this scenario. Any week off, illness, or stretch of bad meetings creates a forecast gap with no redundancy. This is the strongest argument for adding an AE.

Churn assumption is the variable to watch. The forecast holds if monthly gross MRR churn stays at $10K or below. A jump to $20K still produces growth, but it cuts December exit MRR by roughly $70K. CS performance through year-end is the trustline.

Sales comp is not the leakage. Across every scenario in this band, sales comp stays under 15 percent of gross profit. Investment dollars belong in expansion and closing capacity.

Where This Model Breaks

The AE might not ramp. Industry standard is 25 to 30 percent of new AEs miss ramp in their first 6 months. A 90-day check-in with clear PIP triggers is non-negotiable. Forecast assumes a normal ramp.

Cannibalization risk. Handing warm inbound to the AE means Chris closes harder deals. Personal close rate may dip in months 1 to 3 before AE is autonomous. Plan the optics around it.

Churn doubling cuts the upside in half. The AE adds revenue input. If monthly churn climbs from $10K to $20K, December exit MRR drops by roughly $70K. The AE hire pays for itself in either case but the growth story dulls. CS performance still matters even when sales scales.
